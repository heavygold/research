= DAF XF
:nofooter:

== Introduction

DAF (van Doorne's Aanhangwagen Fabriek) is dutch heavy vehicles manufacturer founded in 1928 and part of the American PACCAR group since 1996 <<b4>>.

== Marks

[id=m1a,reftext=Mk. 1a]
=== Mark 1a

1987 -> 1991

DAF 95

Engines: 310, 350 or 380 hp <<b4>>

[id=m1b,reftext=Mk. 1b]
=== Mark 1b

1991 -> 1997

DAF 95

Engines: 329, 364, 401 hp. Later Cummins N14 with 507 hp was added too <<b4>>.

[id=m2,reftext=Mk. 2]
=== Mark 2

1997 -> 2003

DAF 95 XF (the "XF" are following the "95").

XF = eXtra Force

About 80% new components compared to DAF 95 <<b3>>.

12.6 liter, 6 cylinders engines with 380, 480 or 530 hp. At of begining the 530 version was Cummins N14 also used in 95.500 - later replaced with the DAF 12.6 engine) <<b3>>

Available cabs:

* Comfort cab
* Space cab
* Super Space cab

Day cab not available on this model and forward <<b3>>.

Almost 79,000 95XF were built <<b3>>.

[id=m3,reftext=Mk. 3]
=== Mark 3

2003 -> 2006

DAF XF 95 (the "XF" are in front the "95").

[id=m4,reftext=Mk. 4]
=== Mark 4

2005 -> 2013

XF 105 Introduced and replaced the XF 95 from 2007.

Recieved Euro 6 engines in 2012

[id=m5a,reftext=Mk. 5a]
=== Mark 5a

2012 -> 2017

Called just "XF" (with no following number)

[id=m5b,reftext=Mk. 5b]
=== Mark 5b

2017 -> 2021

Facelift

[id=m6,reftext=Mk. 6]
=== Mark 6

Practically a new truck

The largest cabs are designated XG and XG+
